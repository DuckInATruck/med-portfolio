## Topic
History taking — abdominal hernias
Focus: Differentiating reducible vs strangulated epigastric hernias

## Key Learning Points
- Reducible hernia: contents return to abdomen spontaneously or with manual pressure; no vascular compromise
- Strangulated hernia: vascular supply to hernial contents is compromised; surgical emergency
- History red flags for strangulation: sudden onset pain, inability to reduce, systemic features (fever, vomiting, tachycardia)
- Epigastric hernias occur through linea alba between umbilicus and xiphisternum; often contain omentum or pre-peritoneal fat
